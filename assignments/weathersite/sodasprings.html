Soda Springs - A Destination

by Shan'l Parish

Soda Springs, Idaho is set in Caribou County and is named for the hundred of natural springs of carbonated water that are located in its vicinity. The Soda Springs Geyser was created when the town settlers drilled into the ground for a swimming pool and the geyser erupted. Now a prominent tourist attraction, the 100 foot geyser is released every hour for your viewing pleasure. Soda Springs is the home for over 3000 residents. The weather in this area fluctuates from up to 83 degrees F for the summer high, down to 8 degrees F for the winter low. They get an average of 16 inches of rainfall a year, which is about half of the US average. Soda Springs gets an extremely high amount of snowfall per year, averaging 72 inches. . . . Because of Soda Spring's extreme annual snowfall, snowmobiling is a popular pasttime. This area has more than 250 miles of snowmobile trails through the surrounding mountains. Also available within Arthur Kelly Park are beautifully groomed Nordic ski and snowshoeing trails. In the summertime, there are many historical places to visit including Oregon Trail wagon ruts remnants, Kelly Park, and Sheep Rock Point. Camping and hiking are a popular pasttime.
