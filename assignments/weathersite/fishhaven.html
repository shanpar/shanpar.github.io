Little Fish Haven, Idaho is a Little Bit of Heaven on Earth

by Shan'l Parish

Fish Haven, a small undiscovered community near Bear Lake sits at the foot of the mountains on the Idaho side of US Highway 89. It is not official a city or a town and only 50-100 people actually live there. It is a beautiful place to take your family during the summer months, with camping, hiking, and water-play. Many residents live in Fish Haven in the summer months and then move to Logan or surrounding areas for the winter. Summer temperatures average around 84 degrees F and July is the hottest month in Fish Haven. In the winter, Fish Haven is known for its heavy snowfall, averaging 57 inches of snow per year, almost twice the US average. Winter temperatures are cold, getting as low as 10 degrees F. Bear Lake State Park draws a huge crowd during the summer months.

Vast beach areas, boat and skii-do rentals, waterskiing and fishing are big attractions for the lake for many . . . visitors. In the surrounding mountains there is ATV trails, bird watching, caves to visit and even horse back riding. The old Pickleville Playhouse is a destination you don't want to miss as well. In the winter months the ground is covered with heavy snowfall which lends itself to over 350 miles of snowmobile trails.
