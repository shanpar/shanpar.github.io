Preston, Idaho - A Great Place to Visit, A Great Place to Live

by Shan'l Parish

Preston is located in the beautiful Cache Valley and is surrounded by farmland and rolling hills. It is a small town community of just over five thousand residents, but is experiencing consistent growth over the past two years. The Preston area experiences mild summer temperatures ranging from 78-86 degrees F with cool nights with lows in the mid-40's. Fishing, hiking and water-skiiing are popular recreational activities for residents here. Preston city has two city parks and shops that line the downtown area. Preston is an area that experiences high snowfall, averageing 11-16 inches of snowfall each month for December through February. The average annual snowfall is 54 inches which makes for great snowmobiling. The Preston Hill Climb occurs every year in January where spectators can watch some of the best snowmobilers in the country. Preston gets an average annual precipiation of 17.48 inches per year. For more climate information, click here.

Preston, Idaho also offers rich historical educational opportunities. Visit the Bear River Massacre site where solders attached a band . . . of 450 Shoshoni Indians. See the home of Napoleon Dynamite and tour the town where the movie was filmed. Don't forget to make a date the first week of August with the famous Preston night rodeo. It is an event the whole city celebrates with sidewalk sales, craft vendors and antique fairs. There is a Kid's Festival, live entertainment, a parade, and even a Wild West Shoot Out. If you are in to cycling, there are expansive country roads with minimal traffic to ride on. Join the Little Red Riding Hood women's bike race in Cache Valley.
